The Thai Constellation is a slow-growing, variegated variety of Monstera deliciosa. Its striking leaves are broad and fenestrated (naturally split), with creamy-white variegation distributed like stars across a night sky. Plant in well-draining aroid mix and provide a moss pole for support.
Watering: Allow top 3–4 cm of soil to dry before watering.

Light: Thrives in bright filtered light. Avoid direct midday sun which can scorch the variegated tissue.

Humidity: Loves high humidity. Use a humidifier or pebble tray.

Fertiliser: Feed monthly with balanced liquid fertiliser during growing season.

Care Guides

Simple plant care that actually works.

Use these basics to keep your indoor plants healthy, stable, and growing well through the seasons, whether you are a beginner or a collector.

☀️

Light

Bright indirect light is safest for most indoor plants. Move slower-growing plants a little closer to a window.

💧

Water

Check soil first. Water when the top layer feels dry instead of watering on a fixed schedule every week.

💨

Humidity

Leaf-pleasing humidity helps tropical plants. A tray, humidifier, or grouped plants can make a big difference.

🐛

Pest check

Look at the underside of leaves weekly. Catching pests early keeps the plant healthier and easier to treat.

🗓️

Weekly care rhythm

Monday: check soil. Wednesday: wipe leaves. Friday: inspect pests. Weekend: rotate the pot for balanced growth.

🧪

Troubleshooting

Yellow leaves usually point to overwatering, while crispy edges often mean dry air or too much direct sun.

Plant FAQs
How often should I repot?

Most plants are repotted when roots fill the container or growth slows noticeably.

Should I fertilise immediately?

Wait until the plant settles in first, then feed lightly during active growth.

Can I water from the top?

Yes. Top watering works well for most houseplants as long as the pot drains properly.
